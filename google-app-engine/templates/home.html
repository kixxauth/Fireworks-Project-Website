Customized software is expensive and complicated. Right?

We thought so too, but then we started thinking...

What if we could create a sort of factory that would allow us to build simple and affordable software, made to order just for you?

Our factory idea started out by borrowing some ideas from Henry Ford, who created an assembly line to build affordable cars in 1908.

First, we created the common nuts and bolts used to connect software components together.

Next, we designed all the components to be interchangeable so we can easily add or remove functionality, even after the the system is already in use.

Most importantly your software needs to be easily affordable and delivered quickly just like Henry's Model-T cars. So we're building an assembly line that will allow us to quickly assemble all the your parts into a simple system without breaking the bank.

One of the best things about made to order software is that you'll be able to personally choose all the features you need without getting stuck with stuff you don't. Some of the possibilities include customer management, billing, document collaboration, calendars, contact lists, and project management.

You could even include mobile device apps in your system for extra functionality or convienence. And, your new system will also allow you to easily move your information between other systems and applications. It will all just work.

So, why doesn't anyone else build a software factory like this?

Frankly, because it is a very hard thing to build. But, we believe that nothing worth doing comes easy.

Our factory is not quite operational yet, but it is very close. We're working day and night to get ready for our grand opening.

In fact, we've been working so hard that, regretfully, one of us forgot to call his mom on Mother's day. We apologize to moms everwhere and promise to get more sleep and eat healthier as soon as we're done.
