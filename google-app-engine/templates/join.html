Redefine your job.

Working as a member of The Fireworks Project is not like any other job you've ever had. Our company was built from the ground up to be an employee owned and employee managed corporation. In fact we are officially referred to as a "member owned corporation" because our members are a unique blend of shareholders and employees.

We didn't want venture capitalists and other investors dictating our livelihoods, and our unique corporate structure put control of the company into the hands of the people who are building it. The downside is that we don't get paid for our efforts until the company is making money. We feel that is a small price to pay for the freedom to have fun doing what we love to do and get a life away from work to enjoy the stuff that really matters.

This opportunity is not for everyone, but it might be for you.

Become a member of The Fireworks Project.

Make sure you understand our operating agreement below and then submit the membership form at the bottom of this page.

LIMITED LIABILITY COMPANY OPERATING AGREEMENT

Fireworks Technology Projects, LLC (the "Company")

Member-Managed Limited Liability Company

Each Member of the Company agrees to be bound by the following provisions:

Work Product.

All Members irrevocably transfer and assign all right, title and interest to any work product created (now or in the future) in the course of Company projects to the Company. In creating any work product for the Company or otherwise performing services for the Company, Members shall be independent contractors of the Company.

Point Allocation.

At the end of the first full calendar quarter following the creation of the Company (the "Initial Period"), the founding member(s) (the "Founder(s)") shall determine the number of Points to be allocated to each Member of the Company (including the Founder(s)) with respect to contributions made prior and up to the last day of that quarter. At the end of each month thereafter, Active Members, as defined below, or their designated representatives shall determine the number of Points to be allocated for the month then ended.

Point Allocation Cap.

The total number of points allocated from any Active Member to any other member during a one month period may not exceed the total number of points held at the end of the previous month by the allocating member.

Points Received.

Points received by each Member will be cumulative throughout the life of the Company. Members who have received Points during the most recently completed month shall be deemed "Active Members".

Payment of Revenues.

The Active Members shall decide the amount of revenues to be paid out to Members ("Member Payments") during any month, including upon any sale or dissolution of the Company. Member Payments shall be paid to Members in proportion to the number of Points held by each Member as a percentage of all Points outstanding. From time to time, the Active Members may decide to multiply all Points by a factor designed to adjust the number of Points in light of the size of the membership entitled to receive Points or for other reasons.

Unless otherwise decided by the Company, all Member Payments shall be deemed compensation to the Members for their contributions to the Company as independent contractors.

Liability of Members.

The debts, obligations and liabilities of the Company are solely the debts, obligations and liabilities of the Company. A Member will not be personally liable for a debt, obligation or liability of the Company solely by reason of being or acting as a Member or Active Member.

Board of Directors.

The Board of Directors (the "Board") of the Company shall consist of 3 Directors with a decision of 2 Directors controlling. The Board shall be appointed and removed by Members holding a majority of the Points held by all Members voting on that decision. The Board shall be elected annually. No Director shall be compensated for his or her services to the Company as such unless agreed by the Active Members. The business and affairs of the Company shall be managed by or under the direction of the Board and the Company may, by decision of the Board, authorize agents to sign documents or otherwise act on its behalf. The Board shall have the power to perform all acts which it may deem necessary or advisable for the Company, but no Director acting alone shall have the power to bind the Company. The Directors and other agents of the Company shall be indemnified and held harmless from and against any and all claims, expenses, costs, and liabilities which such person may incur in connection with his or her duties on behalf of the Company, to the maximum extent permitted by the Vermont Limited Liability Company Act (the "Act").

Decisions by Members.

Other than decisions to be made by the Active Members under other parts of this Agreement, a decision of the Members holding a majority of the Points held by all Members voting on a specific matter shall be a decision of the Members (except as to amendments to this Agreement, as described below). The following decisions need approval by the Members: (i) election of the Board or removal of members of the Board, (ii) amendments to this Agreement or the Articles of Organization of the Company (approval of Members with 2/3 of the Points of all voting Members required), (iii) sale of all or substantially all of the assets of the Company, and (iv) dissolution of the Company. The Board shall adopt such procedures as it considers reasonable to ensure a fair opportunity to vote by Members on specific matters.

Founder(s) Agreement.

During the Initial Period, the Founder(s) may cause the Company to enter into an agreement with the Founders on such terms as are agreed upon by the Founder(s) and are disclosed to the Members.

Tax Election.

The Company shall elect to be treated for federal and state income tax purposes as a "Subchapter C corporation". The Founder(s) or their designee(s) shall be the shareholder(s) (the "Shareholder(s)") of the Company for federal and state income tax purposes.

Choice of Law.

The law of the State of Vermont shall govern this Agreement without reference to its conflicts of law principles.

Arbitration.

The Members agree that any and all disputes related to or arising from this Agreement will be resolved through online arbitration under the rules of and as facilitated by Virtual Mediation and Arbitration System, LLC.

Non-transferability.

Any interest of a Member in the Company created under this Agreement will be non-transferable and shall terminate upon the death of the Member.

Registered Agent.

Gravel and Shea is the Company's registered agent in the State of Vermont. The registered agent's office is located at 76 St. Paul Street, Burlington, VT 05401.

Designated Office.

The location of the principal place of business of the Company will be the office of the Company's registered agent.

Termination of Access and Active Membership.

A Member's status as an Active Member, may be terminated by vote of a majority of the votes cast by voting Active Members within 2 weeks after such termination is proposed by any Active Member.

Contracts With Third Parties.

With the exception of the agreement between the Company and the Founder(s) described above, no Member or any other person may sign a contract on behalf of the Company unless expressly authorized by a decision of the Board to do so, and any contract signed without such express authorization will be invalid.

Duty of Loyalty.

Because it is the expectation that the Members will be free to engage in pursuits outside of the Company, the Members may devote such time to the Company as they deem appropriate and may engage in such activities outside of the Company as they wish to pursue, including those which may compete with the Company.

Integration.

This Agreement constitutes the entire operating agreement among the parties.

ACKNOWLEDGMENT OF ARBITRATION.

This Agreement contains an agreement to arbitrate. After entering into this agreement, I understand that I will not be able to bring a lawsuit concerning any dispute that may arise which is covered by the arbitration agreement, unless it involves a question of constitutional or civil rights. Instead, I agree to submit any such dispute to an impartial arbitrator as described above.
